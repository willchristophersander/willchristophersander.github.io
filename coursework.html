STAT2510B Applied Probability

Statistics

Probability and statistical inference for modeling random events and uncertainty in real-world systems.

Course description

In order to model a real-world phenomenon in a realistic manner one must allow for the possibility of randomness. Probability provides the framework for describing and understanding random events. Statistics combines the art of making sense out of data and the science of making decisions in the face of uncertainty due to randomness.

We will discuss a variety of theory and applications including topics in combinatorics, conditional probability, random variables, mathematical expectation, limit theorems, and statistics.

The class will consist of recorded lectures (viewed prior to class), in-class group work, discussion, and projects to highlight particular topics.

  • Combinatorics, conditional probability, and Bayes' rule
  • Discrete and continuous distributions
  • Random variables, expectation, and limit theorems

CS1640A Discrete Structures

CS Theory

Discrete math foundations for computer science: logic, proofs, and formal reasoning for algorithms.

Course description

Introduction to analytic and formal methods of computer science with practical examples, including logic and proofs, sets and functions, primes and divisibility, analysis of data structures, recursion relations, and proof methods.

After completing this course the student will be able to work comfortably with logic, apply proof methods, use proper vocabulary, operate with data, decipher and solve word problems, organize logic puzzles, manipulate wording to form structural arguments, evaluate data, and communicate concepts.

  • Logic, sets, functions, and proof techniques
  • Divisibility, recursion, and discrete modeling
  • Quantitative reasoning and problem solving
